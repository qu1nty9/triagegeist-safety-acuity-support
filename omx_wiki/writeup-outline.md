## Title

Triagegeist: A Safety-Focused Acuity Support Baseline for Emergency Department Triage

## Subtitle

Predicting ESI-style triage acuity while explicitly measuring dangerous under-triage.

## 1. Clinical Problem Statement

Emergency triage is a high-pressure workflow where clinicians must prioritize patients with incomplete information. The most clinically concerning model failure is under-triage: assigning a patient to a less urgent category than their true acuity, especially for ESI 1-2 patients.

This project builds a reproducible decision-support prototype that predicts triage acuity from intake data and chief complaint text, then evaluates performance with safety-focused metrics.

## 2. Data

Use the synthetic Triagegeist emergency department dataset:

- 80,000 training records.
- 20,000 test records.
- Structured intake data, vitals, demographics, chief complaint text, and comorbidity history.
- Target: `triage_acuity`, ESI-style 1-5 where 1 is most urgent.

State that the data is fully synthetic and not real patient data.

## 3. Methods

Preprocessing:

- Joined all tables on `patient_id`.
- Removed train-only outcome columns `disposition` and `ed_los_hours` from modeling.
- Encoded categorical variables.
- Imputed numeric vitals and retained missingness indicators.
- Treated `pain_score == -1` as not recorded.
- Vectorized chief complaint narratives with TF-IDF.

Model:

- Balanced sparse linear classifier.
- Fixed stratified validation split.
- Submission predictions generated by refitting on the full training set.

## 4. Evaluation

Report:

- Accuracy.
- Macro-F1.
- Ordinal MAE.
- Quadratic weighted kappa.
- Confusion matrix.
- Severe under-triage rate for true acuity 1-2 predicted as 3-5.

Key result:

- Full valid feature baseline: 0.9946 holdout accuracy, 0.9840 macro-F1, 0.9975 QWK, and 0 severe under-triage in the validation split.

## 5. Findings

Main findings:

- Chief complaint text carries very strong acuity signal in this synthetic dataset.
- Missingness of vitals is clinically informative and concentrated in lower-acuity records.
- Mental status, GCS, oxygen saturation, respiratory rate, shock physiology, and NEWS2-style severity are strongly aligned with high acuity.
- A model can look excellent on global metrics while still needing safety-focused validation.

## 6. Limitations

Limitations:

- The dataset is synthetic.
- Holdout validation is not prospective clinical validation.
- Text signals may be more regular than real triage notes.
- The model should be interpreted as a research prototype, not a deployable medical device.
- Real deployment would require calibration, external validation, clinician workflow testing, monitoring, and governance.

## 7. Reproducibility

Include:

- Notebook runs end-to-end.
- Random seed fixed.
- All data joins are by `patient_id`.
- No train-only outcome columns are used as model features.
- Generated submission follows `sample_submission.csv`.

## 8. Impact

The useful impact pathway is a safety layer for triage:

- highlight likely high-acuity patients,
- reduce variation in triage scoring,
- surface cases needing immediate clinician review,
- audit under-triage patterns across sites and patient subgroups.
